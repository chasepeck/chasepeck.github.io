Nature to Space

The cows march in
Through the metal door
Sick of being blamed
For the gas

The fish follow suit
Gaily hoping for
A more delectable life
A diet without trash

Along stomp the elephants
Anxious, gazing around
Looking for those crosshairs
They are too familiar with

Somehow the trees walk up
Most of them circumcised
One-halves, one-thirds, and one-fourths
Spitefully refuse to make oxygen

Gliding in, the birds
Take their last glance
Of the land below
Somberly reminiscing at the past

The dolphins spring in
Tisk-tisking, one thinks
Everybody underestimates
My intelligence

In summation
The flora and fauna
Flutter and flounder
Faithfully feeling fine.

*

The microbes, well
Have no idea what is happening
Slide on the ship
With the crowd

Here comes the Sun
Mildly annoyed
Gives the planet one last flare
Before engulfing it

A certain primate arrives
Homicidal, horny, and on fire
Barely sets their hand on the handle
When the ship takes off

The course will be set
Far, far from here
Away from the things
That sent nature up

Up, up into the heavens
They wave goodbye
To Man whom they left on the ground
Who will be going down to Hell.
